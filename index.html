Our Projects

Educational opportunities for Dropout children in Tamil Nadu

We are dedicated to ensuring continuous education for dropout children in over 80 villages within Tamil Nadu's Dindigul district, particularly in Oddanchathiram, Palani, Kodaikanal, and Thoppampatti blocks. Our mission is to provide drop-out children with their basic right to a secure future through continuous education. We achieve this by offering bridge classes, tutorial support for classroom subjects, imparting basic life skills, and providing nutritional supplements for holistic growth. Since 2005, we have empowered 6,500 underprivileged children from 80 villages with quality education. From 2005 to 2015, we successfully implemented the Government of India's SSA (Sarva Shikshan Abhiyaan) program, mainstreaming over 500 dropout children each year. After 2015, we have continued this program through funds generated from our own efforts. Causes Driving Our Interventions:

a) Limited Parental Support: Many parents in these villages are unable to assist their children with their studies. The new approach to education, which requires home-based assistance, poses a significant challenge for children in rural and tribal areas, as they cannot afford private tuition. Since our formation in 2002, REEDSAT has been advocating for their cause.
b) Poor Educational Standards: The educational standards among children in these sections are alarmingly low.
c) Low Awareness Levels: General awareness on crucial daily life matters such as personal hygiene, road safety, environment, child trafficking, and climate change is inadequate.
d) Malnourishment: Many Tamil Nadu children, especially those in rural areas, suffer from malnutrition and are underweight. This hampers their academic performance, concentration, and attentiveness in classrooms.
e) Forced Migration: Parents often migrate in search of work due to successive years of drought conditions, leaving their children behind in the care of relatives. This separation negatively impacts the children's education and overall well-being.
f) Involuntary Migration: Many children are left to grow up with parents working in distant towns, driven by unemployment, social discrimination, and natural disasters. This involuntary migration exposes them to various risks, including early school dropout, child labor, and health issues.

Our mission is to support these "home alone" children, ensuring they receive proper food, education, care, and protection from abuse. Together, we can make a difference in their lives and build a brighter future for these vulnerable children

Women Empowerment:

Empowerment of Women At REEDSTA, we are committed to empowering women in Tamil Nadu by focusing on several key areas:

a) Sensitization on Women's Rights: We believe in raising awareness and promoting the assertion of women's rights.
b) Capacity Building: We provide essential skill training to enhance women's capabilities and self-confidence.
c) Financial Inclusion: Our efforts include facilitating credit linkages with banks and other financial institutions to ensure financial inclusion for women. In Tamil Nadu, women, especially those in rural areas, have traditionally been deprived of their rights. They often bear the entire responsibility of their families while dealing with challenges such as alcoholism and irresponsible behavior among men, which further worsen their situation.

Our interventions aim to empower women and shape the future of adolescent girls. Our primary objectives include:

  • Formation of Self Help Groups (SHGs): Currently, we have registered 4,944 women as members, organized into approximately 412 SHGs. Each group consists of 12 members who contribute to the group's savings. They meet regularly, receive training in financial management, income generation, and micro-entrepreneurial skills. Discussions cover topics like hygiene, health, STI prevention, nutrition, domestic violence, and economic independence, with legal aid provided when necessary.
  • Promotion of Savings, Micro Credits, and Micro Enterprises: We facilitate credit linkages with banks and financial institutions, enabling women to establish tailoring shops, petty shops, grocery stores, and other businesses that strengthen the rural economy.
  • Vocational and Entrepreneurial Skills Training: We provide training in vocational and entrepreneurial skills, loan management, and micro-enterprise development, empowering women to become financially independent.
  • Inter-SHG Federation Activities: To enhance social and economic independence, women from different villages come together to share knowledge and experiences within our SHGs. This collaborative effort strengthens our mission to empower women across the region.

Together, we are working towards a brighter future where women in Tamil Nadu can assert their rights, gain financial independence, and lead empowered lives

Adult Literacy in Tribal Hamlets

Providing literacy to adults especially the elderly in Tribal Hamlets is crucial because they face daily challenges. They struggle to read bus destination boards when traveling to towns, have difficulty understanding loan agreements from local vendors, and rely on educated youth for help. Our goal is to empower them with basic literacy skills like writing their names instead of using thumb impressions, reading bus route destinations, and understanding vernacular newspapers. Through our Adult Literacy program, we offer basic literacy classes lasting 2 hours each day, led by local volunteers in every village. We supply essential materials such as slates, books, chalkboards, and stationery. This program runs in 45 tribal hamlets in Palani and Kodaikanal blocks of Dindigul District, Tamil Nadu benefiting over 500 individuals. Our mission is to help these adults gain the fundamental literacy skills they need for a better quality of life with dignity.

Free Legal Clinics:

In Kodaikanal, more than 35 tribal villages now benefit from free Legal Aid Clinics organized by REEDSTA. This initiative is a part of our continuous effort to serve the legal needs of people who have limited access to such facilities, ensuring they receive free legal assistance. We firmly believe that lawyers should step in to provide help when the needy require it because their voices deserve to be heard too. In rural areas, people are sometimes deceived into parting with their meagre possessions, often their land, by unscrupulous individuals. We distribute handbills on consumer rights and legal awareness to empower them. These materials help them protect their property rights, freedom of expression, and access to legal recourse in case of disputes within their villages, guarding against exploitation by influential landowners and moneylenders. Accessing government schemes can also be challenging. Each year, we extend free legal support services to over 60 members from these marginalized communities, ensuring they have the assistance they need to safeguard their rights and access justice.
